COVID-19

After more than three years of facing the COVID-19 pandemic head-on, Unity wants to remind our patients and community that the future of this disease remains uncertain. While the situation has improved, there is still a significant risk of contracting the virus, particularly for those who are unvaccinated, people aged 65 and over, and those with chronic medical conditions or who are immunosuppressed. We encourage everyone to stay vigilant by keeping up-to-date with vaccinations, practicing good hygiene, and following public health guidelines. Unity is committed to providing ongoing support and resources to help protect our community's health. Together, we can navigate these challenges and work towards a safer, healthier future.

Unity's Ongoing Commitment to Combating COVID-19 and Promoting Health Equity

Throughout all phases of the District’s pandemic response, Unity has played a pivotal role in combating COVID-19 in communities disproportionately affected by severe illness and death from the virus. As a cornerstone of care within these communities, Unity was uniquely poised to serve as a vaccine distribution partner for the District, championing equitable access to vaccination opportunities for all residents.

In 2021, the success of our team-based care model was evident as we took a leading role in the city’s pandemic response. Our dedicated team mobilized to provide cutting-edge COVID-19 testing, contact tracing, vaccinations, and treatment for our patients. We collaborated with various city agencies and organizations, including the Department of Health, Department of Human Services, Department of Corrections, DC Primary Care Association, and other Federally Qualified Health Centers. These partnerships ensured that our patients and District residents had the necessary information to make informed decisions about the COVID-19 vaccine and treatment options. We proudly hosted mass vaccination events across the city, ensuring access to this lifesaving intervention for all District residents.


In 2022, Unity continued to live its mission of reaching people wherever they are, particularly supporting those experiencing homelessness. In January 2022, in partnership with the Department of Human Services (DHS), we established new Isolation and Quarantine sites to address the heightened needs from the Coronavirus pandemic and support individuals with the Mpox virus. Through Unity’s 340B in-house pharmacy program, providers prescribed and delivered medications on the same day patients arrived at a Unity site. Unity also supported the city’s Pandemic Emergency Program for Medically Vulnerable Individuals (PEP-V), providing site-wide testing and vaccines across the District’s low-barrier shelter sites.


We continue to witness the residual impacts of the pandemic, including heightened mental and behavioral health challenges, increased substance use disorders, and the effects of delayed preventative care. Our entire organization remains committed to advancing health equity, addressing these challenges head-on with the dignity and respect we strive to provide for our patients and each other every day.


Looking ahead, Unity is expanding its outreach initiatives to include mental health support programs and substance use recovery services, ensuring comprehensive care for all community members. We are also enhancing our telehealth services to provide greater accessibility and convenience for our patients. This is where our focus is moving forward, as we continue to navigate the post-pandemic landscape and work towards a healthier future for all.
